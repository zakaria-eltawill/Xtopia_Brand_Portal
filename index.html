03 — Identity

The wordmark

A custom geometric sans — flat-topped strokes, an X with a deliberate gap at its crossing point, a perfectly circular O, and an A resolved to a bare apex with no crossbar. Sharp, structural, architectural — a mark that reads like a plan view, not a script.

The Wordmark

Designed for the built environment.

Each letterform carries a structural logic: the X crosses with intention, the gap at its centre is not a defect — it is a decision. A moment of deliberate breath in an otherwise unbroken form. The O is a perfect circle. The A sheds its crossbar entirely, resolving to a pure apex.

The result works at any scale — 6 mm on a business card or 6 metres on a facade — because it was drawn with precision, not expression.

Construction

Rules, not habits.

The wordmark is built on a modular grid. Cap height defines the unit. All stroke widths are a fixed ratio of the cap height — not visual approximations, but mathematical constants. Nothing is freehand.

Don't

06 — System

Color

Two dominant backgrounds — Deep Navy and Cream — carry the system. Maroon is the signal: used the way a flare is used, rarely, and only at the moment that matters.

Primary dark background Deep Navy #00263E
Primary light background Cream #EFEDE3
Signal — sparing use Maroon #350004
Dark neutral Olive #4F5438
Light accent tint Sage #C1C6A3
Cool accent tint Powder Blue #BFD4DD

The signal rule.

Maroon never appears in body text, tables, or hover states. It marks the one moment XTOPIA is speaking directly — a CTA, an active status, a highlighted number. Used everywhere, it means nothing. Used rarely, it means everything.

Accessibility — Contrast Ratios

All production color pairings must meet WCAG 2.1 AA (4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text).

08 — System

Spacing & Grid

An 8-point base grid governs all spacing decisions across XTOPIA's digital surfaces. Layouts use a 12-column grid with 24 px gutters.

8-Point Grid

Every spacing value is a multiple of 8. This creates predictable rhythm and makes cross-component alignment effortless. The only exceptions are 4 px (hairline) and 12 px (text-level nudge).

10 — System

Motion

Three canonical behaviors carry all animation in the XTOPIA system. Motion conveys meaning — it is never decorative. Every element moving tells the user something about structure, state, or significance.

Entrance
500ms ease-out

Content arrives from below with ease-out. Used for sections loading into view, cards revealing on scroll, and modal content appearing.

translateY(28px → 0) · blur(5px → 0) · opacity(0 → 1)
Emphasis
680ms spring

A spring scale-in with overshoot. Used for hero reveals, key stats appearing, and primary CTA buttons on interaction.

scale(0.55 → 1.14 → 1) · blur(5px → 0) · spring curve
Transition
440ms ease-out

A directional slide for navigation and content switching. Entering from the left signals forward; from the right signals return.

translateX(-48px → 0) · blur(3px → 0) · opacity(0 → 1)

150ms
